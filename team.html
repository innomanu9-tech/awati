Our team

The people behind AWATI’s work.

We are a growing team of agronomists, educators, local facilitators, and community advocates committed to making agri-tech access practical and inclusive.

Leadership

Experienced leaders building trusted pathways for change.

Dr. Amina Boateng

Executive Director

Amina leads AWATI’s strategy and partnerships, bringing deep experience in rural development and agricultural innovation across West Africa.

Nadia Okafor

Programs Director

Nadia oversees field training, climate-smart curriculum design, and the rollout of community-based learning initiatives.

Kofi Mensah

Impact and Finance Lead

Kofi manages monitoring, evaluation, and donor stewardship so every program is measurable and accountable.

Our approach

Local expertise, trusted relationships, and practical support.

Our team works hand in hand with community leaders, educators, and agricultural experts to ensure every initiative is culturally relevant, accessible, and built for long-term growth.

What makes our team effective

  • Deep field experience in women-led agriculture
  • Strong partnerships with local institutions and cooperatives
  • A commitment to transparent, people-centered leadership

Empowering African women to lead resilient agri-tech systems through knowledge, finance, and community action.
